Product Autopsy AI use cases

Product Autopsy AI is designed for people and teams who have created something digital, but are not sure why it is not working or what they should do next.

If you are unsure whether to keep investing, change direction or shut the project down, a Product Autopsy can help you make a clearer decision.

Common situations

Published app with no users

You launched an app, but it has very few downloads, users do not return or people do not understand why they should use it.

We analyze: value proposition, onboarding, target customer, competition and monetization.

Landing page with traffic but no sales

You have visitors, but they do not buy, book, contact you or complete the form.

We analyze: headline, trust, offer, objections, CTA and conversion leaks.

SaaS with no signups

Your SaaS seems useful, but nobody signs up or users leave before understanding the value.

We analyze: niche, real pain, pricing, activation, differentiation and free trial.

Digital idea before building it

You have an idea, but you do not know whether it is worth spending weeks or months building it.

We analyze: problem, market, alternatives, target customer and risk of building something nobody wants.

Course, ebook or info product that does not sell

You created content, but you are not getting sales or the message does not convince the audience.

We analyze: promise, transformation, authority, price, audience and competition.

Old product you may relaunch

You have a stalled, forgotten or low-selling product and you do not know whether to recover it or close it permanently.

We analyze: what can be saved, what should change and whether there is a real opportunity.

Internal tool that could become a product

You built a tool for your own use and wonder whether it could become a commercial product.

We analyze: market potential, packaging, buyer, pricing and value proposition.

Feature-heavy product with little clarity

The product does many things, but people do not quickly understand what it is for or why they should pay.

We analyze: simplification, focus, main message and repositioning.

What decision it helps you make

Relaunch

When the product has a useful foundation but needs better messaging, offer, landing page or commercial plan.

Reposition

When the idea has potential but the current audience, problem or positioning is not the right one.

Pause

When there is not enough data, validation or signal before continuing to invest.

Shut down

When the product has low potential, too much competition, weak perceived value or does not solve an important enough problem.
