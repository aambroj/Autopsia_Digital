Product Autopsy: HabitPro

Fictional product: a mobile app for building personal habits.

Current situation: the app gets downloads, but very few active users and no premium sales.

1. Quick diagnosis

The product is not failing because of a lack of features. It is failing because of a lack of differentiation. The value proposition is too similar to other habit-tracking apps and does not explain why someone should pay for this specific alternative.

Commercial risk: high

2. Problems detected

  • The landing page does not explain a specific benefit in the first few seconds.
  • The target audience is too broad: “people who want to improve their habits”.
  • The premium plan does not offer a strong enough reason to pay.
  • There are too many generic features: calendar, reminders, streaks and statistics.
  • There is no clear transformation story for the user.

3. What can be saved

  • The core product is useful.
  • The app can be repositioned toward a more specific niche.
  • The habit-tracking system can become a guided program instead of a simple task list.

4. Strategic recommendation

We do not recommend shutting the product down yet. We recommend repositioning the offer toward a clearer segment: busy professionals who need to rebuild routine, sleep, focus and energy in 30 days.

5. Suggested new positioning

“A 30-day system to rebuild your routines when you feel you have lost control of your time.”

6. Priority actions

  1. Rewrite the landing page with a more specific promise.
  2. Create a guided 30-day program instead of selling isolated features.
  3. Reduce the number of initial screens.
  4. Add clear use cases: sleep, focus, exercise, study or getting back into routine.
  5. Change the premium plan so it sells an outcome, not a feature list.

7. Final verdict

Recommended decision: reposition and relaunch. The product has a usable foundation, but right now it competes as a generic app. It should become a specific solution with a clear outcome.
